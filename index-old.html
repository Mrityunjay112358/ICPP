OUR STORY

I was ten when I first understood how unequal access to education could be.

A woman who worked in our home brought her daughter with her — a girl my age. I had just come back from school, talking about what I had learned, while she had come to work. When I asked why she wasn't in school, the answer was simple: NO TIME, NO MONEY, and TOO LATE

That contrast stayed with me.

So I started small. I began teaching her whatever I knew, sitting together each day after she finished her work. Over time, something shifted — not just in what she learned, but in what she believed was possible. She began to want school for herself.

That moment grew.

But as the effort expanded, it became clear that teaching alone wasn't enough. Children weren't out of school simply because they hadn't learned — they were held back by financial pressure, unsupported systems, and the belief that they had fallen too far behind.

The Doorsteps Project was built from that realisation.

Not just to teach, but to remove the barriers around learning — so that returning to school becomes possible, sustainable, and lasting.
